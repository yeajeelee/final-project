About Coffee

According to legend, a goat herder named Kaldi was the first person who discovered coffee beans and their benefits. Kaldi noticed that after his goats ate the cherries of a particular tree in the ancient coffee forests of the Ethiopian plateau, they were so full of energy that they didn’t want to sleep at night.

There are many different ways to drink and make coffee. Below are a few popular types and what makes each one unique.

Espresso

Espresso is an intricate way to brew a highly concentrated coffee by using high pressure and finely ground coffee. This process creates a strong, syrupy shot with a layer of foam called crema on top.

Latte

“Latte” in Italian means milk. A latte is milk and espresso combined together. It is known for its creaminess and a less strong espresso taste.

Personal favorite!

Cold Brew

Cold brew is made by steeping coffee grounds in water for several days in the fridge. This creates a smooth, less acidic concentrate with a stronger taste and higher caffeine content.

I personally like it with a flavored cold foam on top!

Drip Coffee

Drip coffee is a very standard coffee that is made by pouring hot water onto ground coffee beans, allowing it to brew while seeping through a filter. You typically use a coffee machine, a filter, and water for this method.

Affogato

An affogato is a dessert coffee where hot espresso is poured onto ice cream. The mix of cold ice cream and hot espresso creates a rich and delicious treat.

Shaken Espresso

A shaken espresso is made by vigorously shaking espresso with ice. This chills the espresso quickly, dilutes it just enough to reduce bitterness, and aerates it to create a light, foamy top layer.

It can be finished with a splash of milk for a smooth, balanced taste.

My current go-to with Nespresso pods!
